I worked on designing a standalone solar power system for a building(New Engineering Building at the College of Engineering, KNUST), focusing on how to reliably meet its daily energy needs using renewable energy. The project involved estimating the total load demand (about 32.8 kWh per day) and then sizing the major components of the system accordingly.

Using PVsyst, I designed a 9.6 kWp solar array with a battery storage system to ensure continuous power supply even when solar energy is unavailable. I also analyzed system performance, including energy production, efficiency, and losses, to ensure the design was both practical and reliable.

One key takeaway from this project was understanding how real-world factors like energy losses, battery limitations, and excess generation affect system performance. It gave me a deeper appreciation of how power systems are designed not just for ideal conditions, but for reliability and efficiency in real environments.
